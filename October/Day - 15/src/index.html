Parallax with TailwindCSS

Experience beautiful parallax effects with smooth scrolling

How the Parallax Effect Works

The parallax effect is created by using multiple layers of images that move at different speeds as you scroll. The background and foreground images, such as the mountains and trees here, are placed on separate layers and positioned absolutely. This allows for a smooth scrolling experience where the background moves slower than the content in the foreground, creating depth and a more immersive visual. TailwindCSS is used to handle the styling of the layout, including text positioning, colors, and responsiveness. The images are set to cover the entire screen using object-cover, and the absolute positioning allows them to stack on top of one another for the parallax effect.
